### Background

My education in markets began not with theory, but with practice. For nearly a decade, the professional poker table was my classroom—a masterclass in applied probability, risk management, and emotional discipline. It taught me to think in odds, not certainties, and to act decisively when conditions are favorable.

That entire education has now converged on building predictive models. The engineer's rigor, the investor's patience, and the poker player's probabilistic thinking all focused on one objective: **finding the signal in the noise**.
